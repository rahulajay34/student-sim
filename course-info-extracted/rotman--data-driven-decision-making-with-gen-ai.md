## About Rotman School of Management

- The Rotman School of Management is a premier institute, part of the prestigious University of Toronto. The business school was formally established in 1950 as the Institute of Business Administration. It was renamed the Joseph L. Rotman School of Management in 1997 in honour of its principal benefactor. It has rapidly grown into a global center for management education, earning consistent recognition for its innovative research and unique approach to leadership development.
- Global Recognition: Ranked #13 in QS world ranking in 2026 and #1 in Canada for the Full-time MBA (QS Rankings) consistently ranked among the world's top research schools.
- Visionary Leadership: Rotman’s mission is to be a catalyst for positive change—rooted in transformative learning, informed by original insight, and inspired by the biggest challenges in business and society.
- Creative Destruction Lab (CDL): Home to the world-renowned Creative Destruction Lab (CDL), a unique program that provides resources and mentorship to massively scalable, seed-stage companies.

## FAQs

### Program Overview

**Q: What is the duration of the program?**

The program runs for 6 weeks, offering a structured journey from analytics and Generative AI fundamentals to data-driven decision making for real business scenarios.

**Q: How much time do I need to commit every week?**

You should plan for about 8–10 hours per week, including live classes, self-paced learning, assignments, and project work.

**Q: Will I receive a certificate upon completion?**

Yes. On successful completion of all academic requirements, you will receive a Certificate of Completion in “Data Driven Decision Making with Generative AI” from the Rotman School of Management, University of Toronto.

**Q: What is the mode of delivery for the program?**

The program is delivered fully online through live interactive classes, supported by a Learning Management System (Masai LMS) where you can access readings, assignments, and class recordings.

**Q: Who will be teaching the classes in this program?**

Sessions will be led by faculty from the Rotman School of Management and invited Industry experts, bringing together deep expertise in AI, analytics, economics, and business strategy.

**Q: Will I be able to interact with the faculty in the live class?**

Yes, most live sessions include a dedicated Q&A segment towards the end, where faculty address selected questions shared by learners. The exact format and level of interaction may vary by session, but there is always an opportunity to get key doubts clarified.

### Eligibility & Admissions

**Q: Who is this program ideal for?**

This program is designed for a wide range of learners, including students and career starters who want to build a career in data-driven decision making and AI, tech professionals looking to transition into AI strategy, analytics, or decision science roles, non-tech professionals and managers who wish to leverage AI and analytics for better business decisions, and entrepreneurs or business owners aiming to build AI-first, data-led organizations.

**Q: What are the eligibility criteria for the program?**

Applicants should have completed at least 12th grade (or equivalent). The program is suitable for graduates and working professionals from both technical and non-technical backgrounds.

**Q: Do I need prior coding or AI experience?**

No prior AI or advanced coding experience is mandatory. Basic comfort with numbers and problem-solving is helpful. The course builds your understanding progressively, with support for learners from non-technical backgrounds.

**Q: What is the admission process?**

The admission process typically involves:
Registration for the program and payment of the Qualifier Test fee

Appearing for the Qualifier Test

Counselling/onboarding discussion for candidates who clear the test

Payment of fees (upfront or via EMI) to confirm your seat

**Q: What does the Qualifier Test assess?**

The Qualifier Test checks your readiness across: Verbal Ability, Logical Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, and Basic Maths

### Fees & Payment

**Q: What are the key fee components?**

The fee structure includes:<br><br><b>Qualifier Test Fee:</b> ₹99<br><br><b>Secure Seat Fee:</b> ₹4,000<br><br><b>Remaining Course Fee (Upfront):</b> ₹71,000<br><br><b>Total Program Fee (Upfront):</b> ₹75,000 <i>(plus applicable GST)</i><br><br>EMI options are also available, with the remaining course fee payable in instalments. **Q: What is the difference between the Secure Seat Fee and the remaining course fee?**

The Secure Seat Fee confirms your seat in the cohort after you clear the Qualifier Test and are offered admission. The Remaining Course Fee is the main program fee, payable either upfront or through EMI with our loan partners.

**Q: Are EMI/loan options available?**

Yes. EMI facilities are available through partnered NBFCs/financial institutions. The tenure, interest rate, and monthly instalment amount depend on the loan partner’s policies and your profile. These details will be explained during counselling and by the loan-processing team.

**Q: Is GST applicable on the fees?**

Yes, GST is applicable over and above the listed fees, as per prevailing government regulations.

### Class Schedule & Learning Experience

**Q: What are the class timings?**

The program is structured to be manageable alongside work or studies. Detailed schedules, including days and timings of live sessions, will be shared with enrolled learners before the batch starts via email and Masai LMS.

**Q: How many hours of engagement are required per week?**

You should plan for 8–10 hours per week, which includes live classes, self-paced learning, project work, and assessments.

**Q: How will the class schedule be communicated to students?**

The class schedule will be shared through the Masai LMS and official communication channels (such as Email/WhatsApp communities).

**Q: What if I miss a live session? Will I be able to access the recording?**

Yes, recordings of the live sessions are generally uploaded to the LMS so you can revisit concepts or catch up on a missed class. However, we strongly recommend attending live sessions for the best learning experience.

### Student Support & Learning Resources

**Q: What kind of support will I receive during the program?**

You will have access to
- A program coordination team for non-academic queries
- Faculty and teaching support through live sessions and structured doubt resolution
- Guidance on projects, assignments, and assessments.

**Q: How can I interact with my peers?**

You will be part of an online cohort, with opportunities to interact through live sessions, group discussions, and project work, as well as via the LMS and community channels.

**Q: What learning resources will be available during the program?**

You will have access to lecture material, pre-reads, case studies, assignments, and class recordings via the Masai LMS to support your learning throughout the program.

**Q: How often will assessments be conducted?**

Assessments will be conducted periodically through module-wise quizzes, assignments, and project evaluations to help you track your progress and apply what you learn.

### Career & Industry Readiness

**Q: How does this program help with industry readiness?**

The program is designed to make you business-ready in AI-driven decision making by helping you:
Build a portfolio of AI and analytics projects

Work on practical, industry-relevant case studies

Gain confidence in using Generative AI and analytics to solve real business problems.

**Q: Does this program guarantee a job or placement?**

No, the program does not guarantee a job or direct placement. Instead, it focuses on making you highly competitive in the job market through strong conceptual understanding, hands-on projects, and exposure to real-world use cases.
